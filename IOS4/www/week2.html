  • Devices: Please list two devices (and some of their features) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

    Nuance TALKS: They offer technology that gives users audio for their ontacts, call identifications, text messaging, web browsing, supprots over 20 languages. The minus is that it only works with Symbian phones. Qualcomm has an eyes free cell phone that allows users to have access to a handful of simple finger movements that can be started at any point on the handset's touch screen. Voice prompts and vibration provide feedback to users, and the UI adapts to usage patterns and preferences.



  • Mobile Apps: Please list two apps (either Android or iOS, or both) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

    Blind Tunes for ios script allows you to select multiple files and click them to add to another playlist or create new playlists from selection. It also allows the user the ability to drag and drop multiple tracks across multiple rows of tracks. The window automatically scrolls for you. You can check and uncheck items as well for customizing playlists for burning to disc or manually managing your library that syncs with an iPod. More features include sorting the column headers of a music library or playlist. You can even reArrange the columns to a specific order. Helpful messages tell you if the column browser is enabled and if so, when hitting enter in the sources area, will take you to the columns if you have them enabled.
